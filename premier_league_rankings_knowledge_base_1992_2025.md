# English Premier League (EPL) – Rankings Knowledge Base (1992–2025)

> **Purpose**: This Markdown file is designed as a **knowledge base for testing AI RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)** systems.  
> **Scope**: English Premier League seasons from **1992–93 to 2024–25**.  
> **Note**: Content is structured, concise, and retrieval-friendly rather than fully detailed match statistics.

---

## 1. Overview of the Premier League

- **Founded**: 1992
- **Country**: England
- **Number of teams**:
  - 1992–1995: 22 teams
  - 1995–present: 20 teams
- **Ranking system**:
  - 3 points for a win
  - 1 point for a draw
  - 0 points for a loss
- **Table ranking criteria**:
  1. Points
  2. Goal difference (GD)
  3. Goals scored (GF)

## 4. Typical League Table Structure

Each season's table usually contains:

- Position (1–20)
- Club name
- Matches played (P)
- Wins (W)
- Draws (D)
- Losses (L)
- Goals For (GF)
- Goals Against (GA)
- Goal Difference (GD)
- Points (Pts)

Example schema:

```text
Position | Club | P | W | D | L | GF | GA | GD | Pts
```

## 5. Relegation and Promotion Rules

- **Bottom 3 teams** are relegated to the EFL Championship
- **Top 3 Championship teams** are promoted:
  - 1st and 2nd: automatic promotion
  - 3rd–6th: playoff winner

## 6. Key Historical Eras

### 6.1 Manchester United Dominance (1992–2013)
- 13 titles under Sir Alex Ferguson
- Consistent top-3 finishes

### 6.2 Big Four Era (2003–2010)
- Arsenal, Chelsea, Manchester United, Liverpool
- Top 4 highly stable

### 6.3 Manchester City Era (2017–Present)
- Tactical dominance
- Record-breaking points totals
